FYI supports local charity, Zarach

  • Twitter
  • Facebook
  • Instagram

By Lauren Bennett

July 29, 2022 | 4 min read

A partnership between FYI and Zarach, a charity committed to helping children and families who are living in poverty, has saved the charity over £1500.

Zarach provides beds and basics to children and families living in poverty across Leeds and surrounding areas. The aim is to give every child the basic resources they need to have a good night's sleep and a meal in their tummy so they can thrive at school.

The charity was established by Assistant Head Teacher, Bex Wilson, in 2017 and has since helped thousands of families. “It surprises people how many kids in Leeds don’t have beds. It surprised me!” said Wilson.

The partnership was formed in 2021 and has been running for the last year. During this, FYI have donated digital and print collateral, such as; flyers, wristbands, donation bucket stickers, donation envelopes, thank you certificates and more to help raise the awareness of Zarach and support its services, which saw a surge in demand since the pandemic.

Jess Sandy, Projects coordinator at Zarach, said: “FYI supported Zarach with printing, fundraising & marketing materials and other bespoke requests to support both our day-to-day running and our larger projects. These products were of amazing quality and directly impacted vulnerable children and families across Leeds and supported our fundraising efforts. Support from FYI has saved us money that can now be used to purchase the beds we include in our bed bundles - helping us to give Every Head a Bed.”

FYI will continue to support the charity. Tom Buxey, Director of FYI, said: “We have supported Zarach in whatever way we can because we see the wonderful work they do in supporting families in our local community. As a local businessman and employer, I would urge other local businesses to support such a worthy cause.”
